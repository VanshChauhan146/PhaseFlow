# PhaseFlow
#PROBLEM STATEMENT

In many residential, commercial, and industrial electrical distribution systems, power is supplied through a three-phase system. Ideally, electrical loads should be evenly distributed across all three phases to ensure system stability, efficiency, and safety. However, in practice, loads are often unevenly distributed, leading to phase imbalance.

#Phase imbalance can cause several problems such as:

.Overheating of transformers and motors

.Increased power losses

.Reduced equipment lifespan

.Voltage fluctuations

.Higher electricity costs and potential system failures

Currently, detecting load imbalance often requires manual inspection or specialized hardware monitoring systems, which may not be easily accessible or affordable for smaller facilities or residential setups.

#Objective

Develop a web-based monitoring platform that detects whether the electrical load is balanced across the three phases (R, Y, B) in real time.
Key Features

#KEY FEATURES OF WEBSITE

1.Collect or receive phase load data (current, voltage, or power) from sensors or input sources.

2.Analyze the load distribution across the three phases.

3.Determine whether the system is balanced or imbalanced using predefined thresholds.

4.Display results visually through dashboards, graphs, or indicators.

5.Send alerts or recommendations when load imbalance exceeds safe limits.

6.Provide historical data and analytics for better load management.

Expected Outcome

The proposed Three-Phase Load Balance Detection Website will provide a smart, user-friendly platform that continuously monitors electrical load distribution across the three phases (R, Y, B) and determines whether the system is balanced or imbalanced.

#OUTCOMES

1. Real-Time Load Monitoring

The system will continuously receive and process electrical data such as current, voltage, or power values from each phase. The website will display these values in real time using graphs, gauges, or dashboards, allowing users to easily monitor the condition of the electrical system.

2. Automated Load Imbalance Detection

The platform will automatically analyze the load values of the three phases and calculate the difference between them. If the difference exceeds a predefined threshold (for example 10–15%), the system will identify the condition as load imbalance and notify the user.

3. Visual Indicators and Alerts

The website will provide clear visual indicators such as:

Green indicator → Balanced load

Yellow indicator → Slight imbalance

Red indicator → Critical imbalance

Users will also receive alerts or notifications when imbalance exceeds safe limits.

4. Data Visualization and Historical Analysis

The system will store historical load data and provide charts and graphs that show how the load distribution changes over time. This allows users to:

Identify patterns in power consumption

Detect frequently overloaded phases

Plan better load distribution.

5. Decision Support for Load Redistribution

The platform can provide recommendations to redistribute loads across phases when imbalance occurs. For example, it may suggest moving certain loads from an overloaded phase to a lighter phase.

6. Easy Accessibility

Since the solution is web-based, users such as electricians, facility managers, or engineers can monitor the system from any device with internet access, making the monitoring process simple and efficient.

#POTENTIAL IMPACT
The implementation of this system can have several important benefits for electrical infrastructure, industries, and energy management.

1. Improved Power System Efficiency

Balanced loads ensure that electrical power is utilized efficiently. By detecting imbalance early, the system helps reduce energy losses and improve overall efficiency of the power distribution network.

2. Increased Equipment Lifespan

Phase imbalance can cause excessive heating in equipment such as:

Transformers

Motors

Generators

By maintaining balanced loads, the system helps protect electrical equipment and increase their operational lifespan.

3. Reduced Maintenance Costs

Continuous monitoring and early detection of load imbalance can prevent major electrical faults. This significantly reduces repair costs, downtime, and maintenance expenses.

4. Enhanced Electrical Safety

Severe load imbalance can lead to overheating, insulation failure, and fire hazards. The proposed system improves safety by alerting users before the imbalance reaches dangerous levels.


#APPLICATIONS
1. Manufacturing and Production Plants

Manufacturing industries use many heavy machines such as motors, compressors, CNC machines, and welding equipment, which run on three-phase power.

Application of Load Balancing:

Ensures equal distribution of electrical load across all three phases.

Prevents overloading of one phase which could damage equipment.

Maintains stable voltage for sensitive machinery.

2. Power Distribution Systems

Electric utilities distribute power to industries and commercial areas using three-phase distribution networks.

Application of Load Balancing:

Balances load between phases in distribution transformers.

Prevents transformer overheating.

Reduces power losses in transmission lines.

3. Large Commercial Buildings

Buildings like shopping malls, hospitals, airports, and office complexes use three-phase systems for lighting, elevators, HVAC systems, and escalators.

Application of Load Balancing:

Distributes electrical load from lighting, air conditioning, and elevators.

Maintains stable voltage across the building.

Prevents breaker trips caused by phase overload.
